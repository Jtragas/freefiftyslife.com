Her Story

Barbara L. Turner is serving two life sentences in federal prison. She was charged and convicted under theories of aiding and abetting and conspiracy, not as a principal actor.

She was not physically present at the robbery or the murder and has stated she was not aware that a firearm would be used.

Her sentence reflects the broad reach of conspiracy law rather than her actual presence at the crime.

Her Transformation

Over the course of decades, Barbara has changed. She has reflected, grown, and worked to become a better person in every way she can.

Those who know her describe a woman of strength, resilience, and compassion for others.

She has not wasted her time. She has prepared herself for the opportunity to return home and live a meaningful, lawful life.

Why This Matters

After more than two decades, Barbara’s continued incarceration raises a simple question: when a person has changed, when they have grown, and when their sentence no longer reflects their actual conduct — what does justice require?

Clemency exists for moments like this.
